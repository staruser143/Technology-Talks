The dashed line in the middle is the key thing to notice — it's not a data flow, it's flagging that these are **two separate, complementary views** rather than one pipeline: the left path is what DataZone gives you natively for free (lineage graph, subscriber view, right inside the Data Portal), and the right path is something you'd build yourself on top of CloudTrail/query logs to get actual "queried X times" numbers.

A couple of things worth calling out that the diagram simplifies:

- The **"CloudTrail & query history" box** is really two different data shapes that need to be joined manually — CloudTrail gives you the Lake Formation grant/permission-check side (who was authorized), while Athena's query history and Redshift's system tables give you the actual SQL execution side (what was run, against which table). Neither one alone tells you "asset X was queried Y times" — you need both, correlated by table/database name.
- The **aggregation layer isn't a managed AWS product** in the same sense DataZone or Lake Formation are — it's a pattern (CloudTrail Lake for SQL-queryable audit history, or a custom Glue table fed by a scheduled job) that you'd own and maintain, which is a meaningfully different operational commitment than the rest of this architecture.
- If your governance requirements are lighter — say, you mainly care about *which assets are popular* rather than exact query counts — DataZone's native subscription and project-level usage auditing (left path) may be sufficient on its own, and you can skip building the right-hand aggregation pipeline entirely.
